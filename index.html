Feathers

Feathers are special to birds. They give color, keep the body warm, and help in flying. They are on wings and tail.

  1. They keep the body warm.
  2. They help in flying.

Types of Feathers

Flight feathers
Help in flying; found on wings and tail; large in size.
Contour feathers
Protect the bird from weather.
Down feathers
Tiny and soft; give fluff and warmth.
Think What would happen if a bird did not have feathers?

Wings

Wings are used to fly ‚Äî but not all birds fly. Flightless birds include penguin (wings as flippers), ostrich, emu, and kiwi.

The shape of wings differs. Eagles and falcons have sharp-tipped wings for speed and flying high. Hummingbirds can hover using their wings.

Beaks (Bills)

Beaks help birds eat, sip nectar, peck wood, or filter water. Shape tells what they eat.

Seeds

Sparrow, Goldfinch

Short cone-shaped beaks for seeds.

Strong and conical to crack hard grains.

Nectar

Hummingbird

Long, thin beaks for probing flowers.

Slender ‚Äústraw-like‚Äù shape helps sip nectar.

Meat

Eagle, Vulture

Hooked, sharp beaks for tearing prey.

Curved like a hook for carnivorous food.

Grains & Fruit

Pigeon, Dove (Parrot short-curved)

Short, slightly flat (not conical) for seeds/fruits.

Good for picking grains and fruits.

Filter

Duck, Flamingo

Flat beaks; flamingo filters out water to keep food.

Edges act like a sieve to catch food in water.

Chisel

Woodpecker

Strong pointed beak to peck wood for insects.

Hard ‚Äúdrill‚Äù to reach bugs in bark.

Claws (Feet)

  1. Birds of prey ‚Äî sharp, strong talons to catch and hold prey (eagle, vulture, hawk, owl).
  2. Perching birds ‚Äî flexible toes, one toe backwards for branches (sparrow).
  3. Swimming birds ‚Äî wide, webbed feet (ducks, flamingos).
  4. Climbing birds ‚Äî two toes forward, two backward (woodpecker, parrot).
  5. Wading birds ‚Äî long, thin legs with spread feet (heron, crane).

Laying Eggs

All birds lay eggs. Eggs can be white, colored, or spotted. They have a hard shell outside, yellow yolk and white protein inside. Ostrich lays the biggest eggs.

How do birds fly?

  1. Flapping makes air move faster above wings; then birds spread wings straight to soar.
  2. Weight and body shape matter. Birds are light, with most weight near the centre of the body.

What is Migration?

Migration is the seasonal movement of birds from one place to another due to food, water, weather and temperature. Many travel very long distances for days to reach preferred places.

Birds of Prey

They hunt mice, rodents and insects. They have powerful eyesight and sharp claws. Examples: owls, vultures, eagles, hawks.

We now know

  • Birds are colourful species present on all continents across Earth.
  • All birds have feathers, wings, beaks/bills and lay eggs.
  • These traits vary by how birds use them.
  • Some birds glide or are flightless (ostrich, penguin).
  • Birds fly using wing flaps and light bodies.
  • Birds of prey feed on animals like mice and insects.
  • Migration is seasonal travel to better places for food, water, and weather.
  • Migratory birds can travel long distances for days.
